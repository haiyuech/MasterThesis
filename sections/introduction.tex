\chapter{Introduction}
\label{sec:introduction}

Model-based control strategies, particularly sampling-based methods such as Model Predictive Path Integral (MPPI) control~\cite{williams2017model}, provide an effective framework for autonomous navigation and racing. These planners explore complex state spaces while satisfying nonlinear constraints through massively parallel forward simulation~\cite{williams2018information}. Closed-loop performance, however, depends critically on the fidelity of the underlying dynamics model. In high-speed off-road racing, this dependency is amplified by unstructured terrains such as grass and dirt~\cite{xiao2021learning}. At the limits of handling, vehicles experience aggressive slip angles and highly nonlinear tire--terrain interactions~\cite{han2024dynamics, lee2023learning}. Conventional kinematic or single-track models fail to capture these effects under rapidly varying friction conditions, which necessitates continuous adaptation of model parameters~\cite{levy2025meta}.

Selecting an appropriate dynamics model for MPPI in these environments exposes a fundamental trade-off among physical fidelity, environmental context, and computational latency. Efficient parametric models, such as the classic single-track model, provide the computational tractability required by large-scale parallel rollouts in MPPI, but lack the expressiveness to capture complex terrain-dependent interactions~\cite{datar2024terrain}. In contrast, overparameterized neural models incorporate high-dimensional sensory inputs such as visual features, enabling adaptation to heterogeneous surface conditions~\cite{karnan2022vi}. However, evaluating such models at every rollout step introduces substantial computational overhead~\cite{williams2017information, salzmann2023real}, reducing the achievable control frequency and partially offsetting gains in predictive accuracy~\cite{lu2025decremental}.

This paper presents PIVOT (Physics-Informed and Vision-based Online Tuning), a unified framework for vision-conditioned dynamics modeling in sampling-based control. PIVOT incorporates high-dimensional visual context through online adaptation of a compact parameterization. The parameter generator is evaluated once per control cycle, and its output is reused across all MPPI rollouts. This design decouples context-dependent parameter generation from dynamics evaluation and enables efficient integration of visual information while preserving real-time feasibility.

To evaluate this formulation, a systematic empirical study is conducted on datasets collected from a 1/5-scale off-road platform. Seven modeling paradigms are trained on real-world interaction data and grouped into three categories:

\begin{itemize}
\item Fixed-parameter physics models: Models whose parameters are optimized offline and remain constant during execution.
\item Physics-informed learned models: Hybrid models that adapt interpretable physical parameters together with residual corrections online.
\item Purely data-driven models: Neural models that generate model weights conditioned on visual context.
\end{itemize}

This study investigates four aspects of dynamics modeling for off-road control. First, it analyzes the level of model complexity required for accurate prediction under nonlinear and rapidly changing terrain conditions. Second, it evaluates the effectiveness of physics-informed online tuning in bridging fixed parametric models and fully learned representations. Third, it examines the role of structural priors in improving generalization and data efficiency under real-world, noisy, and data-limited conditions. Fourth, it characterizes the trade-off between model expressiveness and computational feasibility in real-time control.

\textbf{Contributions.} This work makes three contributions:

\begin{itemize}

\item A structured framework for vision-conditioned dynamics modeling in MPC.  
PIVOT formulates visual context integration as online adaptation of a compact parameterization, where parameters are generated once per control cycle and reused across all MPPI rollouts. This decouples perception from dynamics evaluation and enables efficient use of high-dimensional sensory inputs in sampling-based control.

\item A unified and controlled comparison of dynamics model structures.  
A systematic evaluation across fixed-parameter, physics-informed, and purely data-driven models is conducted under a shared training and inference framework, enabling a controlled study of how model structure influences prediction accuracy and generalization.

\item Insights into the role of structure and parameterization.  
Experimental results indicate that, under real-world conditions with noisy observations and limited data, low-dimensional physics-constrained parameterizations achieve superior generalization compared to high-capacity neural models trained under the same supervision, highlighting the importance of structural priors in data-efficient dynamics learning.

\end{itemize}
